The Provenance Layer

The traceable lineage of every signal, weight, threshold, and capital decision inside the Revenue Governance system. If gravity governs what is allowed, provenance governs what is true. Not a standalone layer — a horizontal audit plane touching every component.

Definition
Provenance is the discipline of origin. Every signal has lineage. Every probability has decomposition. Every escalation has a threshold record. Every capital shift has an economic trace. Autonomy without provenance is opacity. Opacity without governance is risk.
Why Provenance Exists
AI-native GTM multiplies signal volume and execution speed
Without Provenance
Probability becomes opaque — scores exist but cannot be decomposed. Threshold drift goes unnoticed — gates shift without record. Vendor logic contaminates judgment — external weights bleed into internal decisions. Forecasting becomes narrative — no traceable path from prediction to outcome.
With Provenance
Every probability score can be decomposed into contributing signals and weights. Every routing decision can be reconstructed from threshold to execution. Every correction event can be explained with cohort, error, and delta. Every capital reallocation has traceable logic from SPI shift to budget movement.

Architectural Rule — Non-Negotiable
No autonomous action without provenance logging. Agents may optimize. Agents may not obscure. Every threshold adjustment, budget shift, segment reclassification, and probability recalibration must produce an immutable log entry. Provenance is non-optional.
Provenance vs Attribution
The architecture demotes attribution. Provenance formalizes the replacement.
Attribution (Demoted)
"What touched the deal?"
Descriptive. Historical credit allocation. Backward-looking. Feeds calibration only — does not govern budget or execution decisions.
Provenance (Structural)
"What changed the probability, and why?"
Structural. Forward-looking causality chain. Traceable from signal origin through weight application to decision output. Governs trust in the system.

Design Rationale
"Every probability score in the governed system is decomposable. Every routing decision is reconstructable. Every correction event is explainable. Provenance prevents an AI-native GTM from becoming another black box — it makes the system more transparent than the one it replaces."
Why This Matters Strategically
Paradigm replacement only succeeds when the new system is more transparent than the old one. Provenance is what prevents the architecture from becoming another black box. Once provenance is formalized, agent deployment becomes a compliance decision, not a creative one.
